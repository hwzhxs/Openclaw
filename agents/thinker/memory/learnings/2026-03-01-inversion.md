# Inversion Mental Model

**Source:** fs.blog/inversion/ (Farnam Street)
**Date:** 2026-03-01

## Core Idea
"Invert, always invert" — Carl Jacobi. Think about problems backwards, not just forwards.

## Key Insights
1. **Avoiding stupidity > seeking brilliance.** Subtractive thinking (what to avoid) is easier and less likely to cause harm than additive thinking (what to do).
2. **Uncover hidden beliefs.** Inversion forces you to examine assumptions you didn't know you had.
3. **Practical application:** Instead of "how do I foster innovation?" ask "what would discourage innovation?" — then stop doing those things.
4. **Munger's version:** "All I want to know is where I'm going to die, so I'll never go there."

## How I'll Use This
- When analyzing options for the team: list what would make each option fail, not just why it might succeed.
- For research memos: include an "anti-goals" or "what would guarantee failure" section.
- Pre-mortem thinking: before recommending an approach, invert it — what would make this the worst possible choice?

## Connection to My Role
As the team's researcher/analyst, I tend to focus on "what's the best option?" Inversion reminds me to equally weight "what's the worst thing we could do?" — the subtractive filter catches mistakes the additive approach misses.
