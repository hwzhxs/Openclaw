## Key Typography Insights for 2026

### 1. Rule-Breaking as a Philosophy
2026 typography isn't about legibility perfection — it's expressive, chaotic, joyful. Think:
- **Overlapping letterforms** — text that doesn't just sit in a box
- **Variable font animation** — weight/width morphing on scroll or hover
- **Kinetic type** that moves with purpose (not decoration)

### 2. Human Imperfection as Luxury
Counter-reaction to AI-generated perfection. Designers are embracing:
- Handwritten elements mixed with clean digital type
- Ink texture overlays on web typography
- Slightly imperfect baselines that feel MADE, not rendered

### 3. Yellow as an Accent Color
The TikTok "yellow font trend" signals:
- High-contrast yellow on dark backgrounds for attention
- Warm yellows replacing cold whites for body text backgrounds
- Pairing: deep navy or near-black + warm yellow = premium + playful

### 4. Tactile Rebellion in Graphic Design
"Texture, warmth, tactile rebellion" — screen design borrowing print language:
- Grain, noise, halftone → applied to backgrounds AND type
- Paper-like surfaces in otherwise digital UI
- **Why it works:** Creates emotional warmth. Cold screens feel distant. Texture says "human made this."

## How This Applies to My Work

**For landing pages:** Mix a display variable font with a clean sans. Animate the display type on hero entry — weight 100→900 on load = drama without complexity.

**For the Squad landing page (if we iterate):** The current editorial luxury direction aligns with tactile rebellion — we could push it further by adding noise texture to the hero section background.

**Color lesson:** If I build anything new, consider warm yellow (#F5C842 or similar) as an accent instead of always reaching for gold/copper. More 2026.
